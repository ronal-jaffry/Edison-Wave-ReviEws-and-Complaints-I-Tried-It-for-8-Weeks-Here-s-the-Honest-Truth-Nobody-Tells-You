## What's Actually Inside Edison Wave?

[Edison Wave](https://open.firstory.me/story/cmq62u89p00p701yyd6hb4260) isn't a supplement — there are no ingredients in the traditional sense. The "active component" is its audio architecture. Here's what the program is actually built on:

**Alpha-wave binaural beats (8–12 Hz)**
Binaural beats work by delivering two slightly different frequencies to each ear — say, 340 Hz to the left and 350 Hz to the right — so the brain perceives a third tone at the 10 Hz difference. This is meant to nudge neural oscillations toward the alpha frequency range, the brain state associated with relaxed alertness. Chen et al. (*Cureus*, 2025) found that alpha-frequency binaural beats produced a statistically significant reduction in stress scores (p=0.022) in a controlled trial of 63 participants, though the effect on working memory was not significant. Honest note: the effect was real but modest, and the researchers explicitly called for more replication work.

**Frequency Following Response (FFR) mechanism**
The underlying mechanism Edison Wave relies on — the brain's tendency to synchronize neural oscillations to the rhythm of an external auditory stimulus — is documented in peer-reviewed literature. Ingendoh, Posny & Heine (*PLOS ONE*, 2023) published a systematic review of binaural beat stimulation research and confirmed EEG evidence of entrainment in multiple studies, while noting that outcomes are variable across individuals. The science is real; the effect size in any given person is not guaranteed.

**Isochronic tones (layered)**
Some versions of the program layer isochronic tones — rhythmic pulses of a single tone rather than two competing frequencies — alongside the binaural beats. These don't require headphones to perceive (unlike pure binaural beats), which makes them a useful belt-and-suspenders layer. The independent evidence for isochronic tones specifically is thinner than for binaural beats, so treat this as a supporting element rather than a primary mechanism.

**Carrier frequency design (around 340 Hz)**
The carrier frequency — the base tone the beat is built around — appears to be in the 330–350 Hz range based on the technical descriptions available. This sits in the mid-frequency range of human hearing, which research suggests is most effective for perceived beat clarity. There's no peer-reviewed evidence comparing carrier frequencies head-to-head for cognitive outcomes, so this is an engineering decision more than a scientifically optimized one.

**Anyone expecting results in the first two weeks.** If your baseline expectation is that you'll feel a difference within seven days and you're not the patient type, this program will frustrate you and you'll write it off before it's had a chance to work. **People seeking medical treatment for clinical tinnitus or anxiety disorders.** Edison Wave is a wellness tool, not a medical device. The FDA has not evaluated or cleared it for any clinical condition. If you're managing diagnosed anxiety, depression, or pathological tinnitus, this should not replace treatment from a licensed provider — and the creators themselves include that disclaimer on the official site.

**Anyone who won't use headphones consistently.** The binaural effect requires stereo separation — each ear receives a different frequency. Without headphones (or earbuds at minimum), the two tones mix in the air before they reach your ears, and the entrainment mechanism doesn't engage properly. If you're someone who finds headphones uncomfortable or inconvenient for daily use, the product won't work as intended.
